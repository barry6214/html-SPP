Super Power Pack Ingredients

Home
  1. Essential Amino Acids
    • Helps boost athletic performance1
    • aids in building strength
    • supports muscles
    • helps fortify connective tissue
    • contains Branch Chain Amino acids
      • “Branched-chain amino acids (BCAAs) are essential amino acids that can be oxidized in skeletal muscle.”2

      • “BCAA supplementation before and after exercise has beneficial effects for decreasing exercise-induced muscle damage and promoting muscle-protein synthesis”2

  2. Glycine proprionyl-l-carnitine
    • increases nitric acid. Helps produce energy. May increase blood flow. Helps reduce buildup of lactic acid. Stimulates muscle growth.
      • “These findings indicate that short-term oral supplementation of GPLC can enhance peak power production in resistance trained males with significantly less LAC accumulation.”3

  3. Beet Root powder
    • Strengthens muscle contractions. Leads to improved cardio-respiratory endurance. Increases nitric oxide4
      • “Six days of dietary nitrate supplementation in the form of beetroot juice (~0.5 L/d) has been reported to reduce pulmonary oxygen uptake (VO₂) during submaximal exercise and increase tolerance of high-intensity work rates, suggesting that nitrate can be a potent ergogenic aid.” 5

  4. Cordyceps
    • superfood, medicinal mushroom. Adaptogen. Fights fatigue. Good for muscle aches
    • increases vasodilation and facilitates delivery of oxygen to working muscles. Increases energy6

  5. Beta Alanine
    • Helps to build lean muscle and maximizes endurance while decreasing fatigue and improving recovery time.
      • “The popularity of β-alanine stems from its ability to enhance intracellular muscle-buffering capacity thereby delaying fatigue during high-intensity exercise by increasing muscle carnosine content. Recent evidence also suggests that elevated carnosine levels may enhance cognitive performance and increase resiliency to stress.”7

  6. Potassium Aspartate
    • Electrolyte. Helps prevent cramping.Helps prevent dehydration

  7. Alpha Lipoic Acid
    • Helps convert sugars to energy. Assists in the delivery of creatine, carbohydrates and other nutrients to muscle tissue. Prevents and repairs muscle damage
      • “Our data indicate possible effects of ALA supplementation, during intensive training periods result in a reduction of muscle damage, inflammation and an increase of recovery.”8

      • “It is well-defined as a therapy for preventing diabetic polyneuropathies, and scavenges free radicals, chelates metals, and restores intracellular glutathione levels which otherwise decline with age.”9


  8. Acetyl l-carnitine
    • Detoxifies ammonia10,increases bloodflow to the heart and working muscles. Reduces physical and mental stress. Antioxident activity. Helps muscle recovery.

  9. Citrilline Malate
    • Changes to Arginine which helps produce nitric oxide11

    • Several studies have reported that L-citrulline supplementation can enhance exercise performance and recovery.12

  10. Ornithine alpha ketoglarate
    • OKG activity has been associated with its ability to induce the secretion of anabolic hormones. “13

    • “Ornithine alpha-ketoglutarate as a potent precursor of arginine and nitric oxide: a new job for an old friend “13

  11. CoQ10
    • Improves energy. Helps compensate for loss of mitochondria in older athletes. Powerful antioxidant

    • “CoQ10 has properties related to bioenergetic and antioxidant activity; thus, it is intimately involved in energy production and in the prevention of peroxidative damage”14

  12. Magnesium Aspartate
    • One of the most easily absorbed forms of magnesium. Used in many bodily functions

    • Assists in the body’s ability to make use of glucose and is important in the transfer of energy throughout the body

  13. Black Pepper (piperine)
    • Works to enhjance the bioavailability of other supplements

    • Powerful antioxidant

  14. Histidine
    • Needed along with beta alanine to create carnosine. Also present in essential amino acids

    • “Histidine is required as a precursor of carnosine in human muscle and parts of the brain where carnosine appears to play an important role as a buffer and antioxidant. It is synthesized in the tissue by carnosine synthase from histidine and β-alanine”15

  15. Betaine (TMG)
    • Methyl donor supports the muscles, promotes endurance, supports detoxification. Also present in beet component

    • “May increase heat tolerance and provide another avenue of supplementation for those who find that heat stress is a major factor in their work, or training for exercise and sport.”16

  16. Taurine
    • “Energy metabolism is a basic and general process, by which the body acquires and uses energy to maintain normal function, and taurine plays a vital role in energy metabolism. Taurine deficiency may cause a weak energy metabolism and energy metabolism dysfunction. Taurine biosynthetic ability is limited, and its supplementation in the diet can strengthen energy metabolism in muscle performance, cardiac function, liver activity, and adipose tissue.”17
